Vitamin C Singer Biography

Vitamin C (also known as Colleen Ann Fitzpatrick), is an American singer, songwriter, record producer, dancer, and actress who was born on July 20, 1972, in Old Bridge, New Jersey, United States.

Fitzpatrick began her career as an Ivory soap baby and child actress, appearing in John Waters’ film Hairspray (1988), as well as continued to appear in minor roles in films before starting the alternative rock band Eve’s Plum in 1991.

In 1999, she embarked on a solo career under the name Vitamin C, releasing her eponymous debut album Vitamin C (1999), which was certified as Gold and later Platinum by the RIAA. Singles from the record include “Graduation (Friends Forever)” as well as her most successful hit, the Top 20 Gold-certified “Smile”. Fitzpatrick’s second album, More (2001) spawned several singles, including “As Long as You’re Loving Me” and “The Itch”.

Fitzpatrick would return to acting in 2000, appearing in the horror film Dracula 2000 (2000), as well as having cameo appearances in getting Over It (2001) and Scary Movie 2 (2001), and appeared as a panelist on the spoof talent series The WB’s Superstar USA in the year 2004. Fitzpatrick was ranked No. 76 on the Maxim Hot 100 Women of 2001.

Mattel released a Vitamin C doll in 2000. Fitzpatrick was a video game character in the game EA Sports Triple Play by EA Sports. She has her own Tommy Hilfiger lipstick color developed after her signature yellow and orange hair. On March 21, 2012, she was appointed as Vice President of Music at Nickelodeon.
