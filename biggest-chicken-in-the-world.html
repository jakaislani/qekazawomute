Biggest Chicken In The World

June 2024 · 4 minute read

Discover The Biggest Chicken In The World:

Chickens exhibit a broad range of sizes.

While the majority of breeds typically weigh around 6 pounds, larger chicken varieties can tip the scales at a hefty 8 to 15 pounds.

Orpingtons and Brahmas stand among the most favored larger breeds.

These gentle giants boast both beauty and charm, making them a top choice for many poultry enthusiasts.

Nevertheless, numerous other sizable chicken breeds also exist.

Top 10 Biggest Chicken In The World:

10. Orpington

Although they are the smallest breed on this list, Orpington hens still impressively reach 8 pounds, while Orpington roosters can weigh 10 pounds and stand at 16 inches in height.

Their broad, fluffy feathers make their already large bodies seem even more substantial, and their soft, giant stature is a joy for anyone to embrace.

However, it’s essential to monitor their weight, as Orpingtons have a hearty appetite.

Excessive weight gain can lead to health issues and a decrease in egg-laying productivity.

Beyond their size, Orpingtons make an excellent addition to your family.

They embody all the desired traits of a dual-purpose hen.

Hens provide a continuous yield of 3-5 medium-sized eggs per week.

Specifications:

Weight: 8-10 pounds

Height: 16 inches

9.The Australorp

Australia’s answer to the cherished English Orpington, shares the same beginner-friendly traits.

However, Australorps tend to be slightly taller in stature.

Among the grand heritage breeds, they can reach weights of up to 8 pounds.

While they might display initial shyness, they are inherently friendly and will grow more affectionate with time.

Moreover, they possess all the excellent attributes of the Orpington and then some, making them exceptional egg-layers.

In fact, the Australorp is such a source of national pride in Australia that it has been named their National Chicken.

Specifications:

Weight: 8 to 10 pounds

Height: 17 inches

8.Cornish

The Cornish chicken may not be as fluffy as the breeds we’ve previously mentioned, but it more than compensates with its robust muscularity.

This breed boasts a solid, stocky build with a shortened neck.

Cornish chickens take a full 7 months to achieve their maximum weight of 11 pounds.

Despite their substantial size, they feature short legs and a compact, broad body.

Specifications:

Weight: 10.5-11 pounds

Height: 16 inches

7. Cochin

The Cochin, yet another beloved breed, is renowned for its luxurious fluffiness.

Who wouldn’t want to have their very own oversized, cuddly chicken?

Despite their fluffy appearance, which can make them seem larger than they are, Cochins are indeed a sizable breed.

They can weigh up to 11 pounds and reach heights of 22 inches.

Cochins enjoy immense popularity, but caring for a chicken of this magnitude may not suit everyone.

Their substantial size requires more space than smaller chickens, and their hearty appetite can put a strain on your budget.

Specifications:

Weight: 8-11 pounds

Height: 15-22 inches

6.German Langshan

Among the various Langshan varieties, the German Langshan stands out as the tallest, and it’s relatively uncommon in the United States.

Despite their rarity, these chickens are renowned for their impressive height.

The roosters can attain a remarkable stature of up to 32 inches, making them the second tallest on this list.

The heaviest of the German Langshan roosters weigh approximately 9.5 pounds, with hens being slightly lighter.

These chickens produce relatively large eggs in significant quantities, enhancing their practical value for keepers.

Specifications:

Weight: 6.5 to 9.5 pounds

Height: 24 to 32 inches

5. Maline

This exquisite Belgian breed originated from a crossbreeding project in the late 1800s.

Native Flemish Cuckoo chickens were carefully bred with various large chicken breeds found in Belgium at the time, including Langshans, Cochins, and Brahmas.

Surprisingly, the breeding outcomes exceeded expectations, resulting in a new breed that surpassed all the other breeds used in its formation in terms of size.

The heftiest Malines chickens can tip the scales at over 12 pounds!

These birds are celebrated for their prolific egg-laying, producing a substantial quantity of sizable tan-colored eggs.

Additionally, their docile and placid disposition makes them an ideal choice for novice poultry keepers.

Specifications:

Weight: Up to 12 pounds

Height: Not specified
